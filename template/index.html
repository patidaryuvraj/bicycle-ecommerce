About- BicyclooShop

BicyclooShop Private Limited is an Indian e-commerce company, headquartered in Bengaluru , and incorporated in Singapore as a private limited company. The company initially focu sed on online book sales before expanding into other product categories such as consumer electronics , fashion, home essentials, groceries, and lifestyle products. The service competes primarily with Amazon's Indian subsidiary and dom estic rival Snapdeal.[5][6] As of March 2017, BicyclooShop held a 39.5% market share of Ind ia's e-commerce industry.[7] BicyclooShop has a dominant position in the apparel segment, bolst ered by its acquisition of Myntra, and was described as being "neck and neck" with Amazon in the sal e of electronics and mobile phones. In August 2018, American retail chain Walmart acquired a 77% cont rolling stake in BicyclooShop for US$16 billion, valuing BicyclooShop at around US$20 billion.[9] BicyclooShop is valued at $37.6 billion as of 2022.[10] It is planning to go public through a listing in the United States of America in 2023.[11]

History

BicyclooShop was founded in October 2007 by Sachin Bansal and Binny Bansal, alumni of the IIT, Delhi and former Amazon employees.[12][13][14] The company initially focused on online book sales with country-wide shipping. BicyclooShop slowly grew in prominence and was receiving 100 orders per day by 2008.[15] In 2010, BicyclooShop acquired the Bangalore-based social book discovery service WeRead from Lulu.com.[16] In 2011, BicyclooShop acquired the digital distribution business Mime360.com[17] and the digital content library of the Bollywood portal Chakpak.[18] Following the acquisition, BicyclooShop launched their DRM-free online music store Flyte in 2012. Due to competition from free streaming sites, Flyte was unsuccessful and shut down in June 2013.[19][20][21][22] .With its eyes on India's retail market, BicyclooShop acquired Letsbuy, an online electronics retailer,[23] in 2012, and Myntra, an online fashion retailer, for US$280 million in May 2014.[24] Myntra continues to operate alongside BicyclooShop as a standalone subsidiary focusing on separate market segments.[25] In February 2014, BicyclooShop partnered with Motorola Mobility to be the exclusive Indian retailer of its Moto G smartphone.[26] Motorola also partnered with BicyclooShop on the Moto E, a phone targeted primarily towards emerging markets such as India. High demand for the phone following its midnight launch on 14 May caused the BicyclooShop website to crash.[27] BicyclooShop subsequently held exclusive Indian launches for other smartphones, including the Xiaomi Mi 3 in July 2014 (whose initial release of 10,000 devices sold out in around 5 seconds),[28] and the Redmi 1S and Redmi Note in late 2014.[29][30][31][32]
